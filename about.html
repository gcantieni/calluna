Calluna is a young trad trio from western Massachusetts that plays driving dance tunes from an Irish contemporary repertoire. Although barely a year old, the band is quickly making a name for itself through energetic performances and singular tune choices. Performance highlights include playing the main stage at the 2018 New England Folk Festival (NEFFA) in Mansfield Mass., and at the 2018 Portland Triple Decker, DEFFA’s 9-hour contra extravaganza. Calluna performs across New England for contra dances, festivals and concerts.

Jesse Ball (piano, guitar, mandolin, feet) - Jesse spent his earliest years contra dancing in a sling around his parents’ shoulders. Beginning his musical adventures with classical piano, he eventually found his way back to traditional music after drawing influence from jazz, fusion, Andean folk and Indian classical genres. In addition to being a musician he is also a composer whose tunes have found their way into the repertoires of several local folk bands. When he’s not playing music, Jesse can be found contra dancing, working as a sound tech, or growing garlic.

Eileen O’Grady (fiddle) - Eileen grew up amid rich folk traditions in Vermont where she began playing fiddle at a young age under the guidance of local folk legend Pete Sutherland. While in high school she was a member of The Irregulars, a six-piece trad band that performed across New England for dances, concerts and festivals, even taking first prize in the 2011 Young Tradition Contest. After relocating to the Pioneer Valley in 2014 to attend Mount Holyoke College, she became involved in the western Mass. folk scene. She has experimented with and been influenced by many fiddle styles over the years, including Irish, Quebecois, Cape Breton and Old Time. When she’s not playing music or dancing, Eileen is a mild-mannered newspaper reporter.

Gus Cantieni (tenor banjo, mandolin) - As a youth, Gus Cantieni watched contra dances from the safety of his fort (which he constructed from a piano case and a sleeping bag) and fell asleep to the thump of dancers’ feet. After learning the piano, mastering the electric guitar and flirting with the bagpipes, he settled on tenor banjo and mandolin, and began attending Irish sessions and playing for dances across New England. When he's not playing music, Gus cannot be found: he hacks in secret.
